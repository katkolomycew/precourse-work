Me & _nology

My adventure with tech started when I was old enough to sorta-kinda understand Windows XP (but when I was still too young to stay home alone). My dad usually always took me to work, to a company whose entire IT sector depended on his skills. So there I was, a small girl in the big new world of HTML and CSS and JavaScript, watching magic happen on a computer screen.
A decade later I found myself on Tumblr watching the same magic – but this time the computer screen was a lot flatter, and I was the magician. I read and corrected code templates more often than wrote them, but I was still in love with the job, and now I was also realising something else.
Technology will always be everywhere.
It had been present in the company my dad used to work for almost 20 years ago, it was present in the building blocks of the new social media sites that were suddenly taking over, and it will be present in another decade when apps and codes and computers play a role in every industry.
It's inevitable, and I applied to _nology so I could be a part of it once it happens – to master another language, to use it for making a change rather than observing it, and to collaborate with people who want this change to be positive for the world.
And you know, I also just want to be able to say that I'm a professional full-time nerd.
